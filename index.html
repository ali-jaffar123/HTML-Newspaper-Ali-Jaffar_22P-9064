Thursday, August 18, 1988

President Zia-ul-Haq Killed in Plane Explosion

Military Aircraft Crashes Near Bahawalpur – 31 Dead

Acting President Assumes Office

Following the tragic death of President Zia-ul-Haq, Senate Chairman Ghulam Ishaq Khan assumed the office of Acting President under constitutional provisions. Officials confirmed that the transfer of power was completed smoothly and without delay. Government representatives emphasized continuity of governance during this sensitive period.

Nation Observes Mourning

The federal government announced ten days of national mourning across the country. National flags were lowered to half-mast at all public buildings. Special prayers were held in mosques to honor those who lost their lives in the tragedy.

Preparations for the state funeral ceremony

Crash Investigation Begins

The presidential C-130 aircraft exploded shortly after taking off from Bahawalpur under unclear circumstances. Witnesses reported seeing a sudden flash followed by a massive fireball in the sky. Rescue teams reached the crash site but found no survivors. Authorities have launched a formal investigation to determine the exact cause of the explosion.

Security High Alert

Authorities imposed Section 144 in major cities to prevent any possible unrest. Security forces were deployed at sensitive installations nationwide. Citizens were advised to remain calm and cooperate with law enforcement agencies.

International Reaction

World leaders expressed shock and sorrow over the tragic incident. Messages of condolence were received from neighboring countries and international organizations. Many leaders emphasized the importance of stability in the region during this critical time.

Chotay Kashtkaron Ke Liye Ehm Ilan

Habib Bank Limited has announced special agricultural financing facilities for small farmers across the country. These schemes aim to support crop production and rural development under easy repayment conditions.
