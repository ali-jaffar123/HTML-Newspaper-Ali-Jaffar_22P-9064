Thursday, August 18, 1988

Multan Edition

4 Safar-ul-Muzaffar 1409 A.H.
  • State of Emergency Declared Throughout Pakistan
  • Ten Days of National Mourning Announced
  • Military High Command on Maximum Alert

President Zia-ul-Haq Martyred in Air Tragedy

C-130 Aircraft Carrying High-Level Officials Explodes Near Bahawalpur

Transition of Power

Following the tragic loss of the Head of State, the Chairman of the Senate, Ghulam Ishaq Khan, has assumed the role of Acting President. In his address to the nation, he emphasized constitutional continuity. The new administration has pledged to hold elections as originally scheduled later this year.

International Shock

Global leaders have sent messages of condolence to the people of Pakistan. The United Nations has expressed deep concern over regional stability. Several neighboring countries have declared short periods of mourning to honor the late President's diplomatic legacy.

General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq (1924 – 1988)

The nation was plunged into darkness today as news confirmed that the Presidential C-130 aircraft exploded shortly after takeoff. Witnesses near Bahawalpur reported seeing the aircraft struggle for altitude before a massive mid-air explosion occurred. All 31 passengers and crew, including the US Ambassador and senior military generals, were killed instantly.

Search and rescue teams reached the site within hours, but the wreckage was scattered across a wide area of the desert. A high-level commission has been formed to investigate the possibility of sabotage. Meanwhile, the capital remains under heavy guard as funeral arrangements are being finalized for the state burial.

IMPORTANT NOTICE

FOR SMALL FARMERS

In accordance with the new agricultural directive, all small-scale cultivators are required to register for the upcoming seasonal subsidy. Please visit your local cooperative bank with identification papers before the end of the month. Special credit facilities are now available for those affected by the recent harvest shortages.

City Briefs

Public transport in Multan has been suspended for the duration of the mourning period. All educational institutions and government offices will remain closed until further notice. Hospitals have been placed on emergency standby to assist the military command.
