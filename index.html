PRESIDENT ZIA-UL-HAQ PASSES AWAY IN AIR CRASH

National Mourning Announced for Ten Days

International Response

World leaders have expressed their deepest condolences regarding the sudden demise of the Pakistani President. The United Nations has issued a formal statement. Diplomatic missions in Islamabad have lowered their flags to half-mast in respect.

Security Alert

High security has been deployed at all major airports and borders. The military has taken over key administrative roles to ensure stability. Citizens are advised to remain calm during the transition of power.

The Bahawalpur Tragedy: A Timeline

The C-130 aircraft took off from Bahawalpur earlier today before losing contact with the control tower. Eyewitnesses reported seeing the plane tilt uncontrollably before crashing into the desert. The investigation team is currently on-site searching for the flight recorder to determine if foul play was involved.

Ghulam Ishaq Khan, Chairman of the Senate, has officially assumed the role of Acting President. In his first televised address, he promised to uphold the constitution. The nation remains in a state of shock as details of the crash continue to emerge.

Notice for Farmers

The agricultural department has released new guidelines for cotton harvest. All local cooperative meetings are postponed until further notice due to the national emergency.

Local Market Impact

The stock exchange remained closed today following the announcement. Analysts predict a period of economic uncertainty as the interim government takes charge of the country's finances.
