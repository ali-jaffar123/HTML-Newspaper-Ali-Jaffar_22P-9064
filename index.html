National Tragedy | 10 Days of Mourning | High Alert Across Country

President Zia-ul-Haq Killed in Plane Explosion

Military Aircraft Crashes Near Bahawalpur – 31 Officials Dead

Acting President Takes Oath

Following the tragic death of the President, Senate Chairman Ghulam Ishaq Khan assumed the role of Acting President under constitutional provisions. He assured citizens that democratic elections will proceed as scheduled. He also emphasized the importance of national unity and stability during this difficult time.

International Response

Leaders from around the world expressed shock and sorrow. The United Nations emphasized regional stability while neighboring countries announced official mourning. Several countries lowered their national flags to half-mast as a mark of respect.

General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq (1924–1988)

The presidential C-130 aircraft exploded shortly after departing from Bahawalpur. Witnesses reported seeing unusual movement before a massive fireball appeared in the sky. Rescue teams reached the crash site within hours.

All 31 passengers on board were killed, including senior military officers and foreign diplomats. An official investigation has been launched to determine the cause. Authorities have promised transparency in the inquiry process.

Security Measures Imposed

Authorities imposed Section 144 in major cities to prevent unrest. Security forces remain deployed across sensitive locations. Citizens were advised to remain calm and cooperate with law enforcement authorities.

City Updates

  • Schools closed for mourning
  • Blood donations requested
  • Government offices shut
  • State funeral preparations underway
